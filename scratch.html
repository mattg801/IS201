Exploring Utah

Recreational Activities in Utah's National and State Parks: Utah's national and state parks offer a diverse range of recreational activities for outdoor enthusiasts. From hiking through stunning landscapes to water sports and wildlife viewing, these parks provide unforgettable experiences for visitors.

  1. Hiking and Backpacking
    • Explore iconic trails in Zion National Park
    • Discover hidden arches in Arches National Park
    • Trek through diverse terrain in Canyonlands National Park
    • Hike the Devils Garden Trail in Arches National Park
    • Walk the Rim Trail in Bryce Canyon National Park
  2. Scenic Drives and Sightseeing
    • Drive the Scenic Drive Road in Arches National Park
    • Take a scenic drive on Bryce Canyon Road
    • Explore the Southern Scenic Drive in Capitol Reef National Park
    • Visit the scenic byway in Dead Horse Point State Park
  3. Water Activities
    • Paddleboarding and kayaking at Sand Hollow State Park
    • Boating and fishing at Jordanelle State Park
  4. Wildlife Viewing and Photography
    • Observe bison and antelope at Antelope Island State Park
    • Spot desert bighorn sheep in Capitol Reef National Park
  5. Stargazing
    • Experience world-class stargazing in Bryce Canyon National Park
    • Enjoy dark skies at Jordanelle State Park, a certified International Dark Sky Park
  6. Winter Activities
    • Cross-country skiing and snowshoeing in Bryce Canyon National Park
  7. Horseback Riding
    • Take guided horseback rides in Bryce Canyon National Park
  8. Rock Formations and Arches
    • See Delicate Arch, Landscape Arch, and other formations in Arches National Park
    • View hoodoos and unique rock formations in Bryce Canyon National Park
  9. Camping
    • Camp at Bryce Canyon National Park
    • Enjoy camping facilities at Jordanelle State Park
  10. Visitor Centers and Education
    • Explore the Visitor Center Museum in Bryce Canyon National Park
    • Attend ranger programs in various national parks
